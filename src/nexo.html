NEXO

Our inaugural high-altitude balloon mission during the 2024 total solar eclipse. Conducted in partnership with the Nationwide Eclipse Ballooning Project (NEBP), sponsored by Collins Aerospace and supported by the Puerto Rico NASA Space Grant Consortium.

Overview

Mission Objective

NEXO (Space Navigator for Exploration and Observation) was LIFTS' high-altitude balloon payload launched on April 8, 2024, during the total solar eclipse as part of the Nationwide Eclipse Ballooning Project (NEBP). The mission launched from Smilin V Scout Ranch in Austin, Texas at approximately 12:30 p.m., collecting flight, atmospheric, and light data while capturing multi-angle eclipse footage for post-processing and accessibility experiments.

The report-defined mission objectives were:

  • Capture photos and video from multiple angles.
  • Test instrumentation for improving telecommunications between aircraft (FlightAware experiment).
  • Convert eclipse light waves into sound for accessibility.
  • Collect meteorological data.

Flight Profile

Mission Timeline

Launch — 12:30 p.m.

Launch from Smilin V Scout Ranch, Austin, TX at approximately 12:30 p.m.; team followed local recovery and FAA coordination procedures.

Ascent Through Troposphere

Payload ascended through cloud layers, recording atmospheric pressure and temperature data.

T+1:15:00

Eclipse Totality

T+1:45:00

Maximum Altitude

Balloon reached approximately 95,000 feet before burst. All systems nominal.

T+4:30:00

Landing & Recovery

Payload landed and was recovered on private property near Tyler, TX (~200 miles northwest of launch). Recovery team verified data integrity on site.

Payload

Scientific Instruments

Payload build: FOAMULAR extruded polystyrene gondola with a black vinyl outer cover. The parachute did not fully deploy on descent, resulting in an impact speed around 15 mph; the payload remained structurally intact and all recorded data were preserved.

Imaging System

3 cameras: Raspberry Pi Camera Module v3 (4608 × 2592) on Raspberry Pi Zero 2W, mounted to capture at 45°, 180°, and 270° for multi-angle stills and video.

Light-to-Sound Converter

Custom electronics to convert light intensity changes during the eclipse into audible sound waves.

ADS-B / FlightAware Receiver

FlightAware Pro Stick (Raspberry Pi compatible) used to test ADS‑B reception. The experiment recorded about 1,502 unique flights, with signals detected up to roughly 55,000 ft and two outliers around 80,000 ft.

Atmospheric Sensors

BME280 (humidity, pressure, temperature), VEML ambient light sensor, dedicated altimeter, and BNO055 IMU for attitude/acceleration measurements.

GPS Tracking

SPOT Trace tracker (updates ~every 2.5 minutes) with a radio antenna tracker as redundancy, plus APRS/cellular backups for recovery operations.

Telemetry System

LoRa radio system for live transmission of sensor data to ground station during flight.

Results

Mission Outcomes

NEXO exceeded all mission objectives. The payload successfully captured high-definition footage of the solar eclipse from an altitude of approximately 95,000 feet, clearly showing the moon's shadow (umbra) sweeping across the Texas landscape. The payload remained intact and data were preserved after recovery.

The atmospheric data collected during the flight showed measurable temperature drops during totality, consistent with predictions. The light-to-sound experiment produced an audio representation of changing light intensity during the eclipse. The FlightAware experiment recorded about 1,502 unique flights, with signals detected up to roughly 55,000 feet (and two higher outliers near 80,000 ft), informing feasibility limits for future airborne/near-space concepts.

The mission also received major local coverage, including TeleOnce, Wapa Noticias, Telemundo, Metro, and Radio Isla, helping expand interest in space science across Puerto Rico.
