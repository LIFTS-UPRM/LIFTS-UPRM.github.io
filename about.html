Our Mission

Exploring the Boundaries of Near-Space

We are dedicated to launching experiments with the goal of advancing space exploration and studying Earth from above, while testing and developing new technologies. At LIFTS, we believe in the power of collaboration and teamwork. Our diverse team of students brings passion and creativity, allowing us to tackle challenging problems in aerospace research.

Future

Expansion and New Horizons

After a successful start, we are looking to expand and undertake new projects. Future goals include developing new high-altitude balloon missions for scientific research and developing the first CubeSat launched by students from the University of Puerto Rico, Mayagüez Campus.

September 11, 2024

Establishment of LIFTS

After the success of our first payload and recognizing the project's potential, we established ourselves as a special project at UPRM. Titled LIFTS (Launch Initiatives for Technologies in Space), we aim to maintain our main objective: the research and development of space technologies, as well as manufacturing and launching the first CubeSat from the Campus. The group is under the mentorship of Dr. Joan Castro and Dr. Amílcar Rincón.

Post-Eclipse 2024

Public Reception and Outreach

The project received significant media attention, with exclusive interviews with Deborah Martorell from TeleOnce, Zamira Mendoza from Telemundo, and Ada Monzón from Noticentro. The team presented at a Hurricane Hunter event in Aguadilla and the Weather Fest at UPRM. Through public exposure, we sought to showcase the project's achievements and motivate young people to become interested in space sciences.

April 8, 2024

Successful Launch During the Solar Eclipse

The team traveled to the Tyler/Austin, Texas region and successfully launched a high-altitude balloon during the total solar eclipse. Missions included capturing synchronized multi-angle imagery, converting light waves into sound for accessibility, testing ADS‑B/FlightAware reception at altitude, and analyzing atmospheric conditions during totality.

January 2024

Foundation and NEXO Project

The team was founded under the mentorship of Dr. Amílcar Rincón Charris with the goal of researching and developing space technologies. With the support of the NASA PR Space Grant Consortium and Collins Aerospace, we began our first project, NEXO, as part of the Nationwide Eclipse Ballooning Project (NEBP).
