CubeSat Program

UPRM's first student-built satellite. A 1U CubeSat designed, built, and operated entirely by LIFTS students.

Scroll to explore
Overview

Program Objective

The CubeSat Program represents LIFTS' most ambitious undertaking: designing, building, and launching the first student-built satellite from the University of Puerto Rico, Mayagüez. This 1U CubeSat (10cm × 10cm × 10cm) will demonstrate that Puerto Rican students have the capabilities to participate in real space missions.

The satellite will carry scientific instruments to study Earth's ionosphere and test new communication technologies. Upon successful launch and deployment, it will operate in low Earth orbit for approximately one year, transmitting data to our ground station in Mayagüez.

Architecture

Satellite Subsystems

Structure (STR)

Aluminum 6061-T6 frame compliant with CubeSat Design Specification. Houses all subsystems and provides mounting points for solar panels.

Electrical Power (EPS)

Body-mounted solar cells with lithium-ion battery pack. Provides regulated power to all subsystems with overcurrent protection.

On-Board Computer (OBC)

ARM Cortex-M4 microcontroller running FreeRTOS. Handles command processing, data management, and subsystem coordination.

Communications (COM)

UHF/VHF transceiver for telemetry and command. Amateur radio frequencies enable global ground station network support.

Attitude Control (ADCS)

Magnetorquer-based attitude control with sun sensors and magnetometers for attitude determination.

Payload (PL)

Ionospheric plasma probe and experimental SDR receiver for space weather research and communication experiments.

Science

Research Objectives

Ionospheric Studies

Study the electron density and temperature of Earth's ionosphere, particularly over the Caribbean region:

  • • Equatorial ionospheric irregularities
  • • Space weather effects on ionosphere
  • • Radio signal propagation studies
  • • Long-term ionospheric monitoring

Technology Demonstration

Test new technologies developed by LIFTS students for future missions:

  • • Software-defined radio in orbit
  • • Student-designed solar panels
  • • Novel attitude determination algorithms
  • • Ground station automation software

Operations

Ground Station

LIFTS is developing a dedicated ground station on the UPRM campus to communicate with our CubeSat. The station will feature automated tracking antennas, a software-defined radio receiver, and mission control software developed by our students.

The ground station will also serve as an educational facility, allowing students to learn about satellite operations and radio communications. We plan to join the SatNOGS network to provide additional tracking coverage for other amateur satellites.

Ground Station Specs

Location UPRM Campus, Mayagüez, PR
Uplink Frequency 145 MHz (VHF)
Downlink Frequency 437 MHz (UHF)
Antenna Type Yagi with Az/El rotator
Software Custom + GNURadio
