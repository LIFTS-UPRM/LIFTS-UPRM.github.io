Overview

Mission Objective

ASCENT (Advanced Science Carrier for Environmental and Near-space Technology) represents LIFTS' second generation of high-altitude balloon missions. Building on the success of NEXO, ASCENT will carry a more sophisticated payload package designed for extended scientific research.

The mission aims to reach altitudes exceeding 100,000 feet while conducting experiments in atmospheric science, radiation measurement, and biological studies. ASCENT will test new technologies developed by our team including improved telemetry systems and an upgraded camera array.

Science

Research Objectives

Atmospheric Analysis

Comprehensive measurement of atmospheric conditions from ground level to the stratosphere, including:

  • • Temperature and pressure profiles
  • • Humidity gradients
  • • Ozone concentration levels
  • • UV radiation intensity

Radiation Studies

Investigation of cosmic radiation at near-space altitudes using custom-built detectors:

  • • Cosmic ray flux measurement
  • • Radiation dose accumulation
  • • Particle energy spectrum
  • • Shielding effectiveness tests

Biological Experiment

Study of biological samples exposed to near-space conditions:

  • • Microorganism survival rates
  • • DNA damage assessment
  • • Temperature stress response
  • • Pressure adaptation

Technology Demonstration

Testing of new technologies developed for future missions:

  • • Upgraded LoRa telemetry system
  • • New GPS tracking module
  • • Improved thermal management
  • • 360° camera system
Payload

Experiment Modules

Primary Sensors

BME280 environmental sensor, UV index sensor, Geiger-Müller tube for radiation detection, and precision pressure transducer.

Imaging Array

Dual 4K cameras for Earth-horizon and zenith views, plus a 360° action camera for immersive footage.

Bio-Payload

Sealed chamber containing bacterial samples with temperature monitoring for post-flight analysis.

Flight Computer

Custom Arduino-based system handling data logging, sensor polling, and telemetry transmission at 10-second intervals.

Communication

LoRa 915 MHz telemetry, APRS for amateur radio tracking, and cellular backup for recovery operations.

Power System

Lithium primary batteries rated to -60°C with integrated voltage regulation and power monitoring.
